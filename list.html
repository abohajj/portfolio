History Of The Former President Muhammad Buhari

Muhammad Buhari (1942-2025) was a Nigerin military leader and dramatically elected president (2015-2023), known for his austere discipline, anti-corruption stance, and militaey background, havimg servedd as Head of State (1983-1985) after a coup before becoming president, winning twice as a democratic leader foocused on security, economy, and infrasture, despite challenges like Boko Haram

Early Life & Military Career

  • He was born on December, ,17, 1942, in Daura, Kastina State
  • He joined the Military Army im 1962, trained in UK,Indian, and US
  • He fought the Nigerian Civil war; become Military Governor of North-Eastern State (1975-1976); Fedral Commissioner for Petroleum.
  • He lead a coup against Shehu Shagari, citing corruption; initiatrd the "War Against Indisipline"

    • Presidency (2015-2023)

  • He was the first opposition candidate to defeat an incumbent president (Goodluck Jonathan) in 20115; re-elected in 2019
  • Tackling Boko Haram insurgency, fighting corruption, and improving infrastructure (roads, rail, power).
  • Invested in agriculture, infrastructure, and oil sector reforms, bringing Nigeria out of recession.

    • Attributes & Legacy

  • Discipline & Integrity: Known for personal austerity, simplicity, and a strong commitment to anti-corruption, earning trust despite being aloof.
  • National Unity: Viewed as a detribalized leader focused on Nigeria's unity, security, and progress.
  • "Buharism": His authoritarian approach in the 80s emphasized order and discipline.
  • Challenges: Faced criticism over handling of insecurity (Boko Haram, kidnappings) and economic recovery pace.

    • Post-Presidency

  • Buhari stepped down in May 2023, completing two terms, and passed away in July 2025, leaving a legacy as a transformative figure who transitioned from military rule to democracy.
